### Deep Research — "The literature review that used to take three weeks."

---

It is a Tuesday evening. You have two hours before your shift. You type your clinical question — plain English, exactly how you would ask your supervisor — into ScholarSync.

**Deep Research** — multi-source literature search across PubMed, Semantic Scholar, and OpenAlex, ranked by semantic relevance and classified by evidence level.

### Learn Mode — "The guide who actually teaches you."

---

You type: "Can you write my discussion section?"

Learn Mode says no.

Not dismissively. Not rudely. It responds: "Let's figure out what your discussion needs to do first. What is the main clinical takeaway you want the reader to leave with?"

You think. You type something. It probes further: "You said the presentation was unusual — what specifically was unusual about the timeline compared to the standard progression?"

You think harder. You type something better.

After two rounds, it gives you a short, direct framework: "A case report discussion needs three things: context in the existing literature, your specific interpretation, and the limitations of your case. Which do you want to start with?"

You start writing. The words are yours.

**Learn Mode** — a Socratic academic mentor that teaches you to write, using your actual paper as the teaching material, and explicitly refuses to write for you.

What this means for you: you do not submit an AI-generated paper and hope no one notices. You submit a paper you actually wrote, that you can defend in a viva, that you can be proud of. And the next paper is easier, because you learned something writing this one.

### PRISMA Systematic Review — "Publication-grade infrastructure on day one."

---

Your department chair has asked you to conduct a systematic review. You know what PICO is. You know what PRISMA is. You do not know how to build a search strategy for three databases, screen 400 papers against inclusion criteria, or generate a PRISMA 2020 flow diagram that meets journal submission requirements.

You enter your PICO: Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome.

ScholarSync generates the full search strategy — MeSH-tagged Boolean queries formatted for PubMed, EMBASE, and Cochrane, ready to paste. No manual query construction.

You upload your retrieved papers. Three independent AI agents screen each one against your criteria. Where all three agree, the paper is included or excluded automatically. Where any agent disagrees, the paper is flagged for your review. The PRISMA 2020 flow diagram updates in real time as you progress.

For every included RCT, Risk of Bias 2 assessment: five domains, domain-level judgments, supporting rationale pulled from the paper text.

**PRISMA Systematic Review** — complete systematic review infrastructure including search strategy generation, triple-agent AI screening, PRISMA 2020 flow diagram auto-generation, and RoB 2 assessment.

What this means for you: the infrastructure work that takes a post-doc six months to build is ready on day one. You spend your time on what actually requires your judgment, not on tasks a structured system can handle.
