### PRISMA SYSTEMATIC REVIEWS
## The pride of submitting a methodologically rigorous review.

A systematic review submitted to an indexed journal must meet PRISMA 2020 reporting standards — the most cited methodological framework in evidence synthesis. This includes a structured search strategy, documented screening process, a PRISMA flow diagram, Risk of Bias assessments, and a data extraction table.

Building this infrastructure manually takes a post-doc researcher six to twelve months.

ScholarSync's PRISMA engine does the infrastructure. You do the science.

- Enter your PICO question. The system generates optimized search strings for PubMed, EMBASE, and Cochrane — properly formatted, ready to paste into each database.
- Upload your results. Three independent AI agents screen each paper against your inclusion and exclusion criteria. Consensus: include. Conflict: flagged for your judgment.
- The PRISMA 2020 flow diagram generates in real time. Every inclusion and exclusion decision is documented. It exports as SVG — submission-ready.
- Risk of Bias 2 assessment. Five domains, per paper, per Cochrane standards. Domain-level judgments with supporting reasoning.

When your guide reads your systematic review, she reads a document that demonstrates methodological discipline. When the journal reviews your Methods section, they see PRISMA 2020 compliance documented across every stage.

**Your systematic review, done at the standard that gets published.**
