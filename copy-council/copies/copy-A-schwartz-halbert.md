### FAQ 5: "I'm doing a systematic review for my MD thesis. My guide says I need PRISMA compliance. What does that actually mean and does ScholarSync handle it?"

PRISMA — Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses — is the reporting standard that journals and thesis committees use to evaluate whether a systematic review was conducted with rigour. PRISMA 2020 (the current version) specifies 27 checklist items covering how you searched, how you screened, why you excluded what you excluded, and how you assessed the quality of included studies.

The practical parts that make PRISMA compliance difficult:

**The search strategy** must be documented in detail — exact search strings for each database, date of search, filters applied. ScholarSync generates this automatically from your PICO question. The output is formatted for direct submission to your methods section.

**The PRISMA flow diagram** must show exactly how many records were identified, how many were screened, how many were excluded and why, and how many were finally included. ScholarSync auto-generates this SVG diagram in real time as you work through screening. It updates with each decision. At the end, you export it — no manual diagram creation, no PowerPoint shapes.

**Screening methodology** must be systematic and documented. ScholarSync's triple-agent AI screening processes your title-abstract list with three independent AI agents, each making an independent include/exclude decision with reasoning. Conflicts are flagged for your human judgment. This is methodologically equivalent to dual-reviewer independent screening with consensus resolution — which is the Cochrane-recommended approach.

**Risk of Bias 2 (RoB 2)** assessment must be conducted for included RCTs. ScholarSync automates this across all five domains, returning domain-level judgments with supporting reasoning from the paper text.

What ScholarSync produces is not a simulation of PRISMA compliance. It is actual PRISMA 2020 compliance — the artifacts, the documentation, the flow diagram — ready to submit with your thesis. Your committee can check the checklist against your submitted document. It will pass.
