The Ethics of Germline Editing in Modern Genomics

The advent of Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats (CRISPR) has fundamentally altered the landscape of genetic engineering. Unlike previous methods, which were often cumbersome and imprecise, CRISPR-Cas9 offers a versatile tool for editing the genome with unprecedented accuracy.

However, the ethical implications of germline editing remain a contentious subject within the scientific community. While the potential to eradicate hereditary diseases is immense, the risk of off-target effects necessitates a robust regulatory framework.

Recent studies by Doudna et al. (2020) suggest that the off-target effects can be mitigated through the use of high-fidelity variants.
