Cardiovascular Risk in Type 2 Diabetes: A Systematic Review of SGLT-2 Inhibitor Outcomes

Sharma P, Gupta A, Mehta R

Abstract

Background: Sodium-glucose co-transporter 2 (SGLT-2) inhibitors have emerged as a promising class of glucose-lowering agents with potential cardiovascular benefits. This systematic review evaluates the cardiovascular outcomes associated with SGLT-2 inhibitors in patients with type 2 diabetes mellitus.[1]

Introduction

Type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) remains one of the most significant global health challenges of the 21st century. Affecting over 537 million adults worldwide, T2DM is intrinsically linked to elevated cardiovascular risk, which accounts for approximately 50% of deaths in diabetic patients.[2]

The advent of SGLT-2 inhibitors — including empagliflozin, canagliflozin, and dapagliflozin — has fundamentally shifted the treatment paradigm. Unlike previous glucose-lowering agents, these drugs have demonstrated cardiovascular benefits beyond glycemic control.[3]
