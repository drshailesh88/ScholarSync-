Consensus: Recent literature (2020-2024) indicates a significant paradigm shift regarding off-target effects in CRISPR-Cas9 applications.

While early wild-type variants posed high risks for germline editing, the introduction of Prime Editing and high-fidelity Cas9 (SpCas9-HF1) has reduced off-target incidence by approximately 85-90% in human cell lines. However, a major bottleneck remains: the lack of longitudinal data regarding p53 activation and subsequent genotoxicity.
