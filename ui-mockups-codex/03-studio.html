The Ethics of Germline Editing in Modern Genomics

The current discourse on germline intervention has shifted from speculative fear to conditional feasibility. Yet that shift can mislead policy if technical progress is interpreted as ethical resolution.

Recent data indicates substantial reduction in off-target events under high-fidelity variants, but the evidence horizon for long-term genotoxic response remains narrow. This gap is not merely technical; it is philosophical. A society cannot treat uncertain intergenerational risk as a tolerable implementation detail.

A stronger policy framing distinguishes capability from permission. Capability asks what the tool can do under controlled conditions. Permission asks what burden of proof is required before irreversible deployment.
